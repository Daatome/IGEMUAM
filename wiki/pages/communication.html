UAM NEWSPAPER APPEARANCES

Our first appearance in a newspaper was in the one from our college, Universidad Autónoma Metropolitana. We had a diffusion of the first version of our project that we presented in the iGEM 2021 Design League Competition. It was a great way to gain notice about the initiative we were building on our road to Paris 2022.We wrote the explanation in the bulletin so that it was understandable for the general public. The interest we started to receive about our project from different people inspired us to continue to try to finance the iGEM 2022 Competition. Then we had an appearance in the Mexico newspaper, Enfoque Noticias, where we participated in an article and also an interview for their Youtube channel. Both experiences were a great way to make dissemination about synthetic biology because it is not a very well-known topic.

SIPBIOS symposium

At the beginning of the season, we were invited to participate in the Symposium of the iGEM Peru UNALM team. Different teams from Latin America participated, the objective was to talk about iGEM and how it has changed our perspective in our academic and personal life. It was interesting to analyze how much we have learned since we entered iGEM and how our perspective on what you can do in science has changed into a multidisciplinary vision with more possibilities. It is very important to promote science and inspire future generations.

Congress of Biotechnology (BIOGENESIS 2.0)

Figure 3. Poster presentation contest at The National Congress of Biotechnology

As part of our efforts to publicize and enrich our project, two representative members of the team, Andrea and Angel, assisted The National Congress of Biotechnology of Mexico (BIOGENESIS 2.0). This event happened in the summer, hosted by the Meritorious Autonomous University of Puebla (BUAP) with a focus on entrepreneurship and synthetic biology. Several iGEM ambassadors assisted at this event and it was a great way to discuss the future of biotechnology. We presented our project, to gain feedback about how we could make it a start-up. Even though we did not win the poster contest, it helped us with feedback in areas of the genetic circuit and how to make the project a business idea. Also, all the presentations at the congress helped us meet people that could be interested in our agriculture project. At the end of the event, we felt inspired to be part of the synbio community and continue through the journey of communicating science.

Social media content creation on Tiktok

Figure 4. iGEM UAM Tiktok channel

We believe that one of the best ways of making science dissemination is through brief interactive videos. That is why at iGEM UAM, we created videos about different scientific topics related to science in general, synthetic biology, and the iGEM Competition. The TikTok platform helped us reach more people than we had ever expected. It is important to be responsible with the knowledge we share. For that reason, we did bibliographic research before uploading a video. Science communication is fundamental in science, and we need to remember that knowledge is useless if it is not transmitted in the right way. We hope that through the creation of this type of content, people all around the world with different backgrounds feel inspired about learning science.

Radio

We were invited to a radio interview after one of our videos went viral where we explained our project and its possible impact. It was incredible to receive a lot of support for our initiative and invitations from different media to continue with the scientific dissemination of what we were building at iGEM UAM. During the radio interview with the Mexico radio station 97.3 FM of UNAM, we were asked questions about the scientific part of our project. Several team members explained it in an understandable and fun way, thanks to the interviewer, Ruth Dafne Jaimes Guerrero. This experience helped us with the diffusion of our project, but also to inspire Mexican students to not give up on the journey of science no matter what the circumstances are in Mexico.

Television

As part of the dissemination and support for the project, the television station "Telediario -Mx" contacted us in May of this year to give us live time during breaking news space. This helped us to have greater dissemination and attribution of the people who have been supporting us. The communication team attended The UAM-Iztapalapa unit where we had a live interview. The questions asked were to publicize the impact of the project, the dates of the final presentation, the coexistence, and the role of society.

Figure 6. Live Interview at UAM-I

Thanks to this type of media dissemination, which had a good rating for us both on social networks and in public. Various chemical companies requested information and contacted us to provide support in kind and money to continue with the experimental part of the project.

Similarly, other iGEM teams, research centers, and educational institutions sought to create bonds of coexistence with us so that we could create an environment of solidarity and support. This was an amazing experience that made us realize the impact we could generate through science communication in the country.

We received an invitation to have an interview with a famous Youtuber, talking about science in Mexico. It was a fun interview and a great way to discuss serious topics that occur in Mexico, such as fake news, lack of financial support in science, the fear of biotechnology, and other controversial topics.

Podcast

We had the opportunity to take part in The Millennial Scientist’s Podcast, in which we discussed the different aims of the competition and how our project offers a solution for a local problem in various Mexican communities. We got the chance of sharing with the audience the challenges that we faced during the past months while exchanging ideas and information with farmers. During the conversation, we talked about the impact that synthetic biology can have on the world and how it will play an important role in how we build the future. At the end of the interview, we were asked about what we would do under limitless–financial and time-wise– circumstances, of course, we took this as an opportunity of sharing where we see our project in the future and the thought of turning it into a reality that would allow cleaner and more sustainable farming conditions for potentially various crops.
