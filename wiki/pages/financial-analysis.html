What does this delta stand for? And what is happening to the money?

Based on the annual production we predicted the amount of resources needed to satisfy one month of production which is going to be kept as inventory in order to prevent the detention of production. This is the working capital and we calculated annually but that number does not represent the real outflow of money per year. Why? In the end of 2022, we have in inventory the working capital of said year and by the end of this year we acquire the working capital needed for the 2023 production. For this, we only add the difference between both years, we acquire the lacking working capital to complete the resources needed for 2023. At the end of 2023 we add the extra working capital needed for 2024 and so forth.

If you read carefully through the numbers, you are going to notice that the difference between annual working capitals is not equal to the ∆. Why is this happening?

As we know, money varies its purchasing power over time mostly decreasing due to inflation. Therefore, we need to consider this change of value. This phenomenon does not only affect the working capital but all expenses.
