Competitors analysis

There are three different methods to use spider venom peptides as bio insecticides, by directly modifying the crops to produce these peptides, to have entomopathogenic fungus or bacteria to produce them or using a cell free system (Windley, 2012). Today, most of the products available are modified entomopathogenic organisms, unfortunately for most of the Mexicans farmers the first option is still to use dangerous agrochemicals.

Similar products available

Some of the available products in Mexico are the ones presented below.

Spear-T Concentrate

The Spear-T Concentrate is a cell free biopesticide produced by the American company Vestaron Corporation. This biopesticide is one of the few cell-free products available and of the even fewer ones that also use spider venom peptides as active ingredients. Like SPIDICIDE-Cx, Spear-T Concentrate uses a fusion protein, in this case, GS-omega/kappa-Hxtx-Hv1a.

This product canât be considered as a competitor of our biopesticide given the fact that it does not target Coleopteran pests. Nonetheless, this is a product worth of our attention as it is the most similar product to what we are designing (Spear-T Liquid Concentrate, 2021).

Metabioss

Metabioss is a biopesticide produced by the Mexican corporation Biamin S.A. de C.V. This product is used to control 200 different species of pests, including the weevil Anthonomus eugenii (Torres, 2012). As many other products available, MetabiossÂ´ formulation is based on the use of an entomopathogenic fungus, in this case, Metarhizium anisopliae. Metabioss uses this fungus spores which need to be resuspended in water for it to be used. (BIOAMIN, 2016a).

This product can be bought in a 250g presentation, available for a cost of $495 MXN or $24.72 USD. (Control BiolÃ³gico De Insectos Metarhizium anisopliae, 2017).

Entonem

Entonem is a biopesticide produced by the Dutch company Koppert Biological Systems which is used for Dipteran, Thysanopteran, Lepidopteran, Hemipteran, and Coleopteran (Otiorhynchus spp.) pest control. EntonemÂ´s formulation contains spores from the entomopathogenic nematodes, Steinernema feltiae, which will target the pests once they reach their larval stage (Koppert, 2022).

This product is available at boxes of 100g and 500g which cost approximately $635MXN and $2,675MXN or $31.98USD and $134.70USD respectively (Field, s.f.).

Malathion

The malathion is an organophosphate insecticide used for the control of a wide variety of pests [PubChem-Source]. This product is sold by many companies and is accessible for most farmers. One of these is the Mexican company, Sanidec, which sells malathion in a unique presentation of 1 liter at $225MXN or 11.33USD (Sanidec, 2010).
