Meeting Itech Toaltepe

Mision

Our mission is to become a global enterprise of high social impact. We aim to improve the life quality of less developed communities through the agroindustrial sector while becoming an international niche for biotechnological development and high-quality agronomic products.

We are also committed to contributing our share to accomplish the 2030 agenda for sustainable development through responsible practices to ensure social, economic, and ecological wellness.

Vision

As young, motivated visionaries of the agronomical sciences, we will provide efficient, sustainable, and high-quality biopesticides based on insecticidal peptides to protect farming. We´ll design novel solutions to tackle pesticide resistance by pests without sacrificing efficiency and accessibility.

Values

The values that define our enterprise and in which we paved our entire project are the ones that follow.

At Itech Toaltepe we are empowered to lay the foundations for sustainable farming.

  • Quality: We consider it essential to provide quality products guaranteeing their efficiency and efficacy.

  • Honesty: We aim to create a trustworthy relationship with our clients.

  • Adaptability: We reckon the necessity of being able to quickly and effectively solve any challenges we might face.

  • Perseverance: We constantly and firmly work to achieve our goals.

  • Respect: We consider it essential to respect equally all people, life forms, and our environment.

  • Solidarity: We protect and preserve natural resources for future generations to use.

  • Awareness: We understand the influence of humankind on the environment and, therefore, aim to generate a meaningful impact.

  • Responsibility: We are committed to using our knowledge and resources to create impactful and ethical solutions to agricultural challenges.

INDUSTRIAL PARKS OF MEXICO STATE

According to the Government of the State of Mexico, 2022, currently, the State of Mexico has 11 parks and industrial zones.

The industrial zone of the State of Mexico is characterized by hosting mostly the automotive, food and chemical industries, distributed mostly in the municipalities of Tlanepantla, Tultitlan, Atlacomulco, Ixtlahuaca, Otzolotepec, Toluca de Lerdo, Lerma de Villada, Cuautitlán Izcalli and Ecatepec.

The parks are managed by private organizations such as FIDEPAR, in which recognized companies operate whose development areas are more prestigious due to the location and services that the industrial park provides.

INDUSTRIAL PARKS OF QUERETARO

In the state of Querétaro, there are 50 industrial parks located mainly in the industrial corridor of the metropolitan area of the capital, including the municipalities of Corregidora, Querétaro and El Marqués (place where there is a production plant of the company Koppert Biological Systems).

The industry within the state of Querétaro is mainly made up of the manufacturing sector. In this case, the automobile and aerospace sectors, followed by the metallurgical and plastics production industries. Only in terms of automotive manufacturing, more than 32 thousand local jobs are provided and there is a production of automotive parts with a value of up to 78,403 million pesos per year. The second sector with the highest value is aerospace, which has been on the rise during the last decade, where 22 infrastructure development projects for the said industry were completed (Vázquez, 2021).

INDUSTRIAL PARKS OF JALISCO

The state of Jalisco has 50 industrial and technological parks. It is one of the most important states in Mexico since it has exports, imports, technology, and food from the industrial zone that the state has. Industrial parks are currently in contact with growth as a job-generating force (Santa Cruz Industrial Condominium, 2019).

In Jalisco, there is production in the commerce, real estate, construction, food, electronics, beverage, chemical, plastic and rubber, tobacco, metal, clothing, and activities industries related to the primary sector. Many of the products derived from these industries are for import and export. That is why many of the areas of opportunity in different areas are in the centre of Jalisco (IIEG, 2021).

INDUSTRIAL PARKS OF JALISCO

Sonora has 49 industrial and/or technological parks distributed in Agua Prieta, Caborca, Cananea, Cajeme, Empalme, San Luis Rio Colorado, Hermosillo, Guaymas, and others.

The main industrial activities include the manufacture of accessories, electronic devices, non-oil mining equipment, the chemical industry, transportation equipment, communication equipment, and measurement equipment (Secretaria de Economía, 2020).

INDUSTRIAL PARKS OF COAHUILA

In the state of Coahuila, there are 25 industrial parks distributed to the southeast, centre - desert, north and the coal zone (Gobierno del Estado de Coahuila, 2022).

Among the main industrial activities is the manufacture of machinery and equipment (22%), basic mechanical industries and manufacture of metal products (9.1%), and production of chemical products (10%), that is why the company Bioamin S.A de C.V. and Sanidec has its facilities in this state.

On the other hand, according to the Doing Business report that classifies economies by their ease of doing business, the city of Torreón, Coahuila ranks 10th in Mexico as a country with the ease of opening a business, 21st in terms of handling business permits of building (INEGI, 2021).

Fiscal

The taxes paid by companies in Mexico are intended to support different public expenses, improve the economy and meet the needs of public education, security and support programs for social and economic development. The body in charge of receiving the payment of taxes is the offices of the Tax Administration System (SAT) and is registered in the Federal Taxpayer Registry (RFC) , this is a process known as "registering with the Treasury".

Companies are identified through a denomination or company name as moral persons.

According to Kontarovsky, 2022, the taxes paid by a company in Mexico in 2021 are the following:

  1. Value-Added Taxes (VAT): Indirect tax levied on consumption, increases the value of a product or service by a percentage. The VAT rate is 16% per month.

  2. Income Tax (ISR): Direct tax applied to profits obtained during a fiscal year. The percentage depends on the taxpayer, it can range from 1.9% to 35% per month.

  3. Special Tax on Production and Services (IEPS): It is the tax that is paid for the production and sale or import of gasoline, alcohol, beer, tobacco, pesticides, chemical products, and foods with high caloric content, among others. This tax is paid annually.

The payment of taxes to companies is made now in which the company begins to have income with the following percentages of costs:

  1. 30% of ISR

  2. 10% of utilities

  3. 10% of PTU (Employee Sahre Ownership).

In case of not paying taxes, some surcharges and fines can be accumulated.

Each factor was assigned a weighting value based on its importance, whose total sum gave 1. In this case, the Demand was considered as the main factor since it implies reducing transportation costs and more direct distribution of the product with our market. On the other end, we selected safety as the last weighted facto.

Then, a score from 1 to 5 was assigned to each state according to the factor being evaluated, where 1=Very bad, 2=Bad, 3=Fair, 4=Good and 5=Very good. These values were multiplied by the weighting value, obtaining a Relative measurement. The relative values of each factor were added together to calculate the rating of each state, and therefore, defining which state is optimal for establishing the plant.

With this method, we decided that the best state to build the plant is the state of Jalisco as it scored the most of all states, with a rating of 3.89.
